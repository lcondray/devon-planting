Design Philosophy

The Pearson Principles

Applied across all six areas ‚Äî a framework, not a formula.

01
Heightened naturalism ‚Äî plants should feel like they belong, as if they emerged from the landscape
02
Layering ‚Äî canopy ‚Üí sub-canopy ‚Üí structural ‚Üí herbaceous ‚Üí ground ‚Üí climbers
03
Stone/plant relationships ‚Äî plants softening, draping over, emerging from stone and stucco
04
Seasonal rhythm ‚Äî overlapping waves of interest, not abrupt seasonal shows
05
Scent as spatial marker ‚Äî fragrant plants at thresholds, gates, entries
06
Structure + looseness ‚Äî clipped forms alternating with loose, naturalistic forms
07
Colour-themed zones ‚Äî each space has a distinct palette and mood
08
Formality near house, naturalism further out
09
Repetition for rhythm ‚Äî key plants repeated across areas for cohesion
10
NC native/adapted species ‚Äî Cotswolds aesthetic translated to Durham climate
